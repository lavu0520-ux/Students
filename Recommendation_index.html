The 3-Question Framework

A professional recommendation must answer:

  • 1. What should be done? (Specific action)
  • 2. Why should it be done? (Based on evidence/analysis)
  • 3. What is the expected result? (The benefit/KPI)

Technical Report Structure

1. Introduction (Purpose of report)
2. Problem Description (The current issue)
3. Analysis/Results (Data & evidence)
4. Conclusion (Summary of findings)
5. Recommendations (Proposed actions)

Professional Example

[Problem] One important issue in university laboratories is high energy consumption. Many machines remain switched on even when they are not being used.

[Recommendation] It is recommended that the department install automatic power management systems. These systems can automatically turn devices off when they are inactive.

[Benefit] Consequently, electricity usage would decrease significantly, reducing costs and making the laboratories more sustainable in the long term.
